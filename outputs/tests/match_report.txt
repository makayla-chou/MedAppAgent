## Final Recommendation
Your GPA of 3.8 is a strong asset and aligns well with many schools. However, your MCAT score of 504 is below the average for most MD programs, especially competitive ones. This will be your main weakness in applications.

**Strategy:**
- Focus on applying to your in-state public schools (Eastern Virginia Medical School, VCU, Virginia Tech Carilion) where your GPA is above average and your MCAT is close enough to average to be considered.
- Include a mix of target and safer public schools with average MCATs in the 506-513 range where your GPA is competitive and MCAT is slightly below average but possibly acceptable.
- Consider private schools with missions aligned to underserved communities and research, especially those with average MCATs near or below your score (e.g., Morehouse, Mercer).
- Avoid applying to highly competitive schools with average MCATs well above 510 unless you have a plan to significantly improve your MCAT or have extraordinary other factors.
- Your strong research, volunteering, and outreach experiences are major strengths and should be highlighted to offset your MCAT.
- Consider retaking the MCAT if possible before the cycle to improve chances at more competitive schools.
- Apply broadly but realistically, prioritizing schools where your stats are at or near their averages and where your career interests align with the school’s mission.

This balanced approach maximizes your chances of acceptance while acknowledging the MCAT challenge.
